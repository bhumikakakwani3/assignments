The sindhis

Sindhi culture

Sindhi culture is rich and distinct, with roots in the Indus Valley civilization. It is influenced by the desert landscape, the Indus River, and the Arabian Sea.

Sindhis worship :
gods and goddesses like Shiva, Shakti, Ganesh, Ram, and Krishna
They also worship Jhulelal, the water god, during festivals like Chetichand and Chaliho
Sindhis are influenced by Sufism, Nanak panth, and Arya Samaj
They are known for being liberal and broad-minded about religion

Traditions:
Sindhis have traditional arts and crafts, including embroidery, which is often worn on special occasions
Sindhi women often wear Gaj, a traditional dress that is embroidered with intricate designs
Sindhi men often wear a cylindrical skullcap with an arch-shaped cut-out

Lifestyle:
Sindhis are known for being practical and flexible
They are known for valuing family, hard work, and honesty
Sindhis are known for their resilience and the spirit of never giving up
